Starting from the kinematic equations for acceleration and the equations derived
from newton's second law, this section will describe how to solve for the
generlized accelerations, $\ddot{\theta}$.

We'll start by manipulating the second law equations to eliminate the
internal forces:

1. Solve eq X for $T_b$, then substitue that into eq Y to elimate to give us our
first equation of motion without the internal forces. 

  $$
  \begin{alignedat}{1}
  m_b\ddot{x_b} &= -T_bs_b \\
  T_b &= -m_b\ddot{x_b}s_b^{-1} \\
  \\
  m_b\ddot{y_b} &= T_bc_b - m_bg \\
  m_b\ddot{y_b} &= -m_b\ddot{x_b}s_b^{-1}c_b - m_bg \\
  (m_b\ddot{y_b} + m_bg)s_b &= -m_b\ddot{x_b}c_b \\
  (\ddot{y_b} + g)s_b &= -\ddot{x_b}c_b \\
  \\
  0 &= c_b\ddot{x_b} +s_b\ddot{y_b} +s_bg
  \end{alignedat}
  $$


2. Solve eq Z for $T_b$, subitute $T_a$ and $T_b$ into eq Q, then substitue the
LHS of Eq B for $-m_b\ddot{x_b}c_b$ to give us our
second equation of motion without the internal forces.

  $$
  \begin{alignedat}{1}
  m_a\ddot{x_a} &= -T_as_a + T_bs_b \\
  m_a\ddot{x_a} &= -T_as_a - m_b\ddot{x_b}s_b^{-1}s_b \\
  m_a\ddot{x_a} &= -T_as_a - m_b\ddot{x_b} \\
  T_a &=  - (m_b\ddot{x_b} + m_a\ddot{x_a})s_a^{-1} \\
  \\
  m_a\ddot{y_a} &= T_ac_a - T_bc_b - m_ag \\
  m_a\ddot{y_a} &= - (m_b\ddot{x_b} + m_a\ddot{x_a})s_a^{-1}c_a + m_b\ddot{x_b}s_b^{-1}c_b - m_ag \\
  m_a\ddot{y_a} &= - (m_b\ddot{x_b} + m_a\ddot{x_a})s_a^{-1}c_a - (-m_b\ddot{x_b}c_b)s_b^{-1} - m_ag \\
  m_a\ddot{y_a} &= - (m_b\ddot{x_b} + m_a\ddot{x_a})s_a^{-1}c_a - ((m_b\ddot{y_b} + m_bg)s_b)s_b^{-1} - m_ag \\
  m_a\ddot{y_a} &= - (m_b\ddot{x_b} + m_a\ddot{x_a})s_a^{-1}c_a - m_b\ddot{y_b} - m_bg - m_ag \\
  m_as_a\ddot{y_a} &= -m_bc_a\ddot{x_b} - m_ac_a\ddot{x_a} - m_bs_a\ddot{y_b} - (m_a +m_b)s_ag \\
  \\
  0 &= m_ac_a\ddot{x_a} +m_as_a\ddot{y_a} +m_bc_a\ddot{x_b} +m_bs_a\ddot{y_b} +(m_a +m_b)s_ag
  \end{alignedat}
  $$


Now we have the following equations of motions that do depend on the internal forces.

$$
\begin{alignedat}{1}
0 &= m_ac_a\ddot{x_a} +m_as_a\ddot{y_a} +m_bc_a\ddot{x_b} +m_bs_a\ddot{y_b} +(m_a +m_b)s_ag \\
0 &= c_b\ddot{x_b} +s_b\ddot{y_b} +s_bg
\end{alignedat}
$$

Note that the equations still depend on the cartesian accelerations of the point
masses. Next, we will use the kinematic equations for acceleration to solve
these equations for the generalized accelerations $\ddot{\theta}$. To make this easier, let
$s_x=s_x$ and $c_x=c_x$.
